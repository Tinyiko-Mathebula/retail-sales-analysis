# Retail Sales Analysis & Customer Segmentation

An end-to-end data analysis project for a UK-based online retailer with 25,000 transactions across 13 months. The goal is to uncover revenue trends, identify top-performing products, and segment customers using RFM analysis to enable data-driven marketing decisions.

---

## Results & Key Findings

- **Total Revenue:** £646,620 across 3,981 customers
- **Seasonal Peak:** November–December accounts for the highest revenue months — Q4 drives ~30% of annual revenue
- **Top Market:** United Kingdom generates 82% of revenue; Germany and France are the leading international markets
- **Pareto Insight:** Top 20% of customers generate 83.6% of total revenue
- **Champions segment** (476 customers) drives the majority of high-value repeat purchases
- **1,248 Lost customers** identified — prime targets for a re-engagement campaign

## SQL Analysis — 10 Business Questions

All queries are in `retail_queries.sql` and can be run directly in MySQL Workbench against `retail_db`.

| # | Business Question | Key SQL Concepts |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | What is the total revenue? | SUM, WHERE |
| 2 | What is the monthly revenue trend? | DATE_FORMAT, GROUP BY |
| 3 | Which are the top 10 products by revenue? | GROUP BY, ORDER BY, LIMIT |
| 4 | Which countries generate the most revenue? | COUNT DISTINCT, GROUP BY |
| 5 | What is the average order value? | AVG, subquery |
| 6 | Who are the top 10 customers? | CASE WHEN, customer tiering |
| 7 | Which day of the week has the most orders? | DAYNAME, DAYOFWEEK |
| 8 | What is the cancellation rate? | Conditional aggregation |
| 9 | Which products have the most repeat buyers? | Subquery, HAVING |
| 10 | New vs returning customers by month? | CTE, JOIN, conditional COUNT |

## RFM Methodology

RFM is an industry-standard customer segmentation technique used by retailers worldwide.

- **Recency (R)** — how recently did the customer purchase? (scored 1–4, 4=most recent)
- **Frequency (F)** — how many orders have they placed? (scored 1–4, 4=most frequent)
- **Monetary (M)** — how much have they spent in total? (scored 1–4, 4=highest spend)

Each customer receives a combined RFM score (max 12) and is assigned to a segment based on their R, F, and M scores. This enables the business to tailor marketing actions to each group rather than treating all customers the same.

# 4. Add your MySQL password to line 30 of retail_analysis_mysql.py
